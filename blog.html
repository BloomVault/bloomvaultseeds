November 2025 — 100 Years of Cannabis Prohibition: Looking Back, Growing Forward

In November 2025, the world quietly marks a century since the first coordinated efforts to outlaw cannabis began. One hundred years of stigma, propaganda, and lost potential — but also one hundred years of persistence, passion, and resilience from growers, healers, and believers who never gave up on the plant.

From Medicine to Menace

Before prohibition took hold, cannabis appeared in American apothecaries as a common remedy for pain, nausea, and insomnia. That changed in the 1930s when political fear, misinformation, and racial bias turned a once-useful herb into a cultural menace. What had been a healing plant became a villain — and the legal consequences lasted generations.

The Long Shadow of Prohibition

By the mid-20th century, prohibition had spread worldwide; in the U.S., the 1970 Controlled Substances Act put cannabis in Schedule I. Communities — especially marginalized ones — paid the price. Yet underground growers responded with ingenuity, quietly preserving and refining genetics. Legendary lines like Skunk, Haze, and Northern Lights weren’t born in labs — they were crafted by dedication and necessity, protected in basements, barns, and hidden valleys.

The Rise of Legalization & Cultural Shift

In the 2000s, reform accelerated. Medical programs laid the groundwork; adult-use markets followed. What began as counterculture is now an agricultural, medical, and lifestyle industry. Still, stigmas linger — research barriers, uneven access, and outdated perceptions. Normalization takes time, and responsibility.

From the Ground Up: A New Era of Growing

For us at BloomVault Farms in Sutherlin, Virginia, this centennial isn’t just a historical marker — it’s a reminder of why we do what we do. Every seed we curate, every cross we make, and every grower we partner with is part of a legacy that survived a century of suppression. Today’s small-batch growers aren’t just chasing potency; they’re chasing purpose — reviving lost expressions, breeding with integrity, and growing with transparency.

Looking Forward

A hundred years after prohibition began, the soil is finally richer with understanding. Growers now have tools, data, and community support that didn’t exist even a decade ago. The next hundred years belong to those who grow with integrity — who document, test, and share honestly — and who keep the focus on the plant, the craft, and the people.

— Written by BloomVault Farms, Sutherlin VA · November 2025 · The Vault Journal
